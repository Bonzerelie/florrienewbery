The Course & Race Structure

  • The Distance: A National B road race covering 98.8 km (61.6 miles).
  • The Circuit (East Hanningfield): Expect a persistently rolling, heavily fatiguing route. Key landmarks include Howe Green, East Hanningfield, Rettendon, Coalhill, and the Beechwood Equestrian Centre.
  • Safety & Escort: Racing on open roads. The National Escort Group (NEG) is providing a mobile safety corridor (not a full road closure).
    Commentary Note: If you hear commissaires sounding their horns, it’s a warning for oncoming traffic.
  • The Prime: Listen for the alarm and bell at the start of Lap 3! This triggers the Prime Lap, with a £50 cash prize waiting for the first rider across the line at the end of the lap.
  • The Finish: Riders will take a specific return route to HQ (turning right onto Main Road down into Rettendon) to avoid double-triggering the MyLaps timing sensors.

The Host Club & The Namesake

  • Southend Wheelers: Recently awarded British Cycling’s prestigious "Focus Club" status—one of only 25 nationwide—recognizing their excellence in rider development and community impact. In 2026, they are heavily expanding their women's and girls' programs.
  • Who was Florrie Newbery? She made history as the very first woman to join Southend Wheelers on February 3rd, 1937. A true trailblazer, she led club runs, rallied local women to race time trials, and became the club's first female committee member.
  • Organiser Shoutout: The event is promoted by Southend Wheelers, with Matt Stock stepping up as both Event Organiser and Safety Officer.

Sponsors

  • Sponsor Spotlight: The highly anticipated Lap 3 £50 Prime is generously sponsored by That Shot Media.

The 2026 BWTC (British Women's Team Cup)

  • Season Opener: The Florrie Newbery Classic is round one of the five-race 2026 BWTC calendar. Teams score points based on their top four finishers.
  • Brand New "Combativity Classification": This is a massive talking point for this season! For 2026, the BWTC is massively incentivizing early attacks.
    • If a breakaway of 8 or fewer riders has a 30-second gap at the half-distance mark, each rider earns +100 points for their team.
    • If a breakaway wins the race with a 30-second gap, that's another +100 points each.
    • The Catch: Even if the mid-race break is caught, they keep those 100 points! Expect explosive racing right from the gun.

Key Racing Points & Riders to Watch

The Narrative: Expect fireworks early. The massive 8-rider London Academy squad will likely launch wave after wave of attacks to get riders up the road for those new Combativity points. Once the early breaks are reeled in on these sapping Essex lanes, the elite engines will take over.

Riders to Watch

  • Georgina Oakley (Loughborough Lightning): The only Elite category rider on the start sheet. Coming off a successful track stint as a tandem pilot, she is recovering from illness but brings incredible pedigree. If she's healthy, she's a massive threat.
  • Emilia Fletcher (London Academy): The outstanding unknown quantity. Coming from a triathlon background, she defaults to time-trial mode. She recently won the Easter Road Race by soloing the final four laps. If she gets a gap, she might just ride away from everyone.
  • Isabella Johnson (Jadan Glasdon pb Vive le Velo): Finished 2nd in this exact race last year, just four seconds off the win. She brings brilliant recent form and is a prime favorite today.
  • Amelia Staunton (Brother UK - On Form): Finished a highly impressive 6th here last year as a junior. Now a 2nd-category senior with a full season in her legs, she will be looking to better that result.
  • Ella Tandy (Simpsons Nouvelles): A 19-year-old dark horse to watch. She’s stepping up from the juniors with a punchy style and a notoriously fast finish.
  • Isabel Darvill (O’Shea Development Team): One of the most experienced riders in the pack. A bronze medalist at the 2024 National Circuit Race Championships, she has raced the Tour of Britain and RideLondon. Tactically brilliant and a major contender if it comes down to a reduced sprint.

Squad Dynamics by Numbers

  • London Academy (8 Riders): They are bringing the largest contingent to the race by a significant margin. The roster features Sasha Dyke (1st Cat), alongside Alice Connor, Emilia Fletcher, Emma Jane Hornsby, Hope Inglis, Lydia Turan, Esme Wiley (all 2nd Cat), and Ailsa McLagan (3rd Cat). This numerical advantage gives them immense power to dictate the pace and send riders into early breakaways to chase the new Combativity points.
  • Wolfox Strykr Le Col RT (5 Riders): The second-largest squad on the day. Featuring Anouska Greenaway, Poppy Kisley, Nadine Oehmcke (2nd Cat), Genevieve Billington, and Sian Reynolds (3rd Cat).
  • Brother UK - On Form (4 Riders): The defending 2025 BWTC champions. Their squad comprises Ellen Bennett, Ellen Inglis, Amelia Staunton (all 2nd Cat), and Lucy Neatham (3rd Cat). They will have to use tactical efficiency to match the larger squads.
  • Paralloy RT (3 Riders): The new team fields 1st Cat Katie-Anne Calton, alongside Millie Skinner (2nd Cat) and Bea Ferry (3rd Cat).
